Introduction

Problem

Post Covid-19 the rules have altered and people have been adjusting to a new normal. Due to some inaccurate information, new altered rules and the extensive travel planning individuals have fund it harder to plan travel.

Solution

The app that we have designed “Travel Round”, aims to provide users with essential travel services, accurate and up to date information and simple features to plan travel.

Tools Used:

Figma, Miro, Unsplash Plugin, Google Drive

My Role & Contribution:

As this is an individual project & case study, my role being a UX Designer Involved -
- Performing all the steps of the Design Process, which includes empathising, defining the problem, Ideation/Brainstorming, Prototyping and Testing. All of these steps were crucial and included the use of the tools provided above.
- The final Prototyping was done on Figma
- Prototype Testing done via Zoom or in person, with the target users.

Problem Statement

User Insight Statement

A busy family oriented physician, who is a mum of two needs affordable accommodation and reliable services because of issues in finding accurate information when planning a trip overseas.

Problem Statement

During the interviews we found that users want to travel to see family and friends, as well as enjoy the experience of visiting a new place. We believe that users want to use a service which is easy, efficient and contains accurate information for optimized & well prepared planning and will help achieve a stress free travel experience and helps us build a platform aid this outcome. How might we support our travellers with receiving the correct information, assistance and functional guidance to improve their trip planning and experience through a more efficient and effective service?

Iterations

Click through below to see the different fidelity stages!

  • Splash Screen, Login/Sign Up Screen and Sign Up Screen

    These are intial sketches of the three screens, where I was trying to figure out the simplest & best layout which stands out to the user. I also wanted a very simple logo, with the app name and a globe to signify the world.

  • Login Screen, Homepage, Package Alert Screen

    These are initial sketches of the the above screens. The login screen is fairly similar to sign up but with slightly different fields, I kept this consistent throught all prototype versions. I wanted the homepage to have a carosel like hero section and have general information about the app with validity that the information is upto date. I wanted there to be a search bar, profile indication icon & a bottom Navigation to easily click through the app. These components are essential for the homepage. I also felt that when the user is new to the app they should get a pop-up alert on the recent travel packages as a way to take them straight to an important feature on the app. The user can also choose to exit this alert if the don't want to explore packages.

  • Packages Screen & Sign Up Successful Screen

    Packages Screen shows all the travel packages which are currently available and on a special price, which are easy to book if users want a simple fixed holiday. The sign up succesful screen is a standard alert that comes after verifying your email to a new account on the app.

  • Splash Screen, Login/Sign Up Screen, Sign Up Screen

    The main difference here is, these wireframes are digital wireframes, they are still low fidleity but they have proper icons and are the main mockup for the final prototype. All fields from sketches plus more have been added to the digital screens, including digitally creating the logo on Figma.

  • Signup succesful screen, Package Alert Screen, Packages Screen

    Same concept with these screens, as they are very close to the sketches, however we can see a more clear layout and alignment in the Packages screen.

  • Login Screen & Homepage

    Digital screens here are also closely replicated to the initial sketches, however I decided to experiements with the nav bar icons to see what suited the menu content, so that the users will easily know how to navigate the app. Links like Forget password which are hyperlinks are made blue & underlined as this is the standard format.

The aim of the user testing at the Low Fidelity Version was to see if Users could complete mini tasks in a straightforward manner. Further testing was done again after the Mid-Fidelity prototype was created.
From the Low Fidelity Testing I wanted to test if the users could Send/Share a booking plan and Favourite a package deal and Login/Sign Up. They were able to complete these tasks successfully, giving feedback that the App is pretty straightforward and the features are efficient.
Some positives included:
- Different options to Login & Sign Up
- Ability to Favourite Deals
- Confirmation of Email
- Ability to Share Plans
- Simplicity and some easy to find features i.e drop down menu
